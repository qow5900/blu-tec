## Who Is Blu-Tec ICT?

Blu-Tec ICT started in 2012 when Raymond Clarke began offering IT support to friends and family in the local community. Over the years it grew into a full-blown hosting and ICT services provider. The company is rooted in what they describe as "honesty, integrity, and continuous improvement" — which, honestly, sounds like every company's mission statement. But given their 14+ years in the industry and the fact that they're still operating as a local, independent provider rather than a reseller of some overseas white-label brand, that reputation appears to have some backing behind it.

Their hosting runs on high-performance servers hosted locally in South Africa, which matters a lot for load times if your audience is South African.

## The Case For Going Local

Blu-Tec ICT is not competing with Hostinger or SiteGround on price-per-gigabyte. They're not trying to. What they offer is something different: a locally hosted, locally supported, Rand-denominated service from a company that has actual roots in the South African community.

A few things that stand out here:

**Billing in ZAR.** No exchange rate surprises. If you've ever had a hosting invoice come through in USD during a Rand slump, you'll appreciate this more than you expect.

**Local servers.** Your website loads faster for visitors in Cape Town, Johannesburg, or Durban. That's just geography. A server sitting in Amsterdam or Texas has measurable latency for South African visitors.

**cPanel and Plesk.** These are the industry-standard control panels. Nothing proprietary, nothing you need to relearn if you ever want to move.

**Free SSL on every plan.** Even the R20 Micro plan includes Let's Encrypt SSL, which means HTTPS is handled without any extra cost.

**Zero setup fees.** Every plan in their store lists "FREE Setup Fee" — you pay the monthly rate and that's it.

## Who Is This Actually For?

**Micro / Basic plans (R20–R30/mo):** Someone who just needs a holding page, a small portfolio, or a simple HTML/CSS site. If you're testing something out, this is a low-risk way to get a proper cPanel hosting environment.

**Standard / Standard Plus (R45–R75/mo):** Small business owners who want a WordPress site, a few email addresses, and don't want to worry about bandwidth quotas. The Standard Plus is the sweet spot if you're running something with actual traffic.

**Advanced / Ultra (R115–R165/mo):** Growing businesses, developers managing client sites, or anyone who's outgrown basic plans. Unlimited bandwidth at these tiers is a meaningful feature.

**Ultra Plus / Platinum (R315–R450/mo):** Presumably for higher-resource setups — the store doesn't publish specs for these two on the plan listing page, so reaching out to their support to confirm what's included before purchasing makes sense.
